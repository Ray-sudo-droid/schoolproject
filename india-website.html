Home

India is one of the most diverse and vibrant countries in the world. Located in South Asia, it is home to over 1.4 billion people, hundreds of languages, ancient traditions, and rapidly growing modern cities. From the snowy Himalayas to tropical beaches, India offers a mix of history, culture, and innovation unlike anywhere else.

This website is your guide to understanding India’s language, money, customs, food, geography, politics, safety, and transportation—everything a first‑time visitor needs to know.

Language(s)

Main Languages

  • Hindi (most spoken)
  • English (used in business and government)
  • 22 official languages total

Basic Hindi Phrases

  • Hello — Namaste
  • Thank you — Dhanyavaad
  • Yes / No — Haan / Nahi
  • Excuse me — Maaf kijiye
  • How much is this? — Kitna hua?

Currency & Visa Requirements

Currency

India uses the Indian Rupee (INR), symbol ₹. Cash is common in markets and small shops, but credit cards and digital payments are widely used in cities.

Visa

Most visitors need a tourist visa before entering India. Many travelers can apply for an e‑Visa online. Passports usually must be valid for at least six months beyond the travel dates.

Culture & Customs

  • Traditional greeting is “Namaste” with hands pressed together.
  • Shoes are removed before entering homes and temples.
  • Modest clothing is expected in religious places.
  • The right hand is used for eating, giving, and receiving items.
  • India celebrates festivals like Diwali, Holi, Eid, and Christmas.

Cuisine

Famous Dishes

  • Chole Bhature
  • Biryani
  • Naan
  • Dosa
  • Idli & Sambar
  • Street foods like Chaat and Vada Pav

Food Etiquette

  • Many people eat with their right hand.
  • Some homes and temples avoid meat and alcohol.
  • It’s polite to taste what is offered.
  • Drink bottled or filtered water to avoid stomach issues.

Geography

  • Located in South Asia.
  • Borders Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar.
  • Major regions include the Himalayas, Thar Desert, Ganges Plains, and tropical coasts.
  • Climate includes summer, monsoon, and winter seasons.
  • Major cities: New Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Bengaluru, Chennai.

Politics, Government & Relations

Government System

India is the world’s largest democracy. It has a parliamentary system with a President as head of state and a Prime Minister as head of government.

Relationship with the U.S.

India and the U.S. have strong ties in trade, technology, education, and defense. Many Indian students study in the U.S., and American companies invest heavily in India’s tech sector.

Relationship with China

India and China are major trading partners but also have border disputes and political tensions. Despite this, both countries remain important economic powers in Asia.

Safety Concerns

  • India is generally safe for tourists, but crowded areas can have pickpocketing.
  • Traffic can be chaotic; visitors often use taxis or ride‑share apps.
  • Drink clean water and choose busy, clean food stalls.
  • Natural risks include heat waves, monsoon flooding, and air pollution in some cities.

Transportation

  • Indian Railways connects most cities and towns.
  • Domestic flights are common for long distances.
  • City travel includes metros, buses, taxis, and auto‑rickshaws.
  • India drives on the left side of the road.

Additional Information

  • Zero was invented over here.
  • Cricket is the most popular sport.
  • India is the birthplace of yoga.
  • Bengaluru is known as India’s tech capital.
